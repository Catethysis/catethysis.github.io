2018

Camera hub for Yandex Self-driving car done by myself in 2 weeks.

Self-driving cars need to see around, and the minimal set is five fish-eye cameras + 1 LiDAR. Our cameras have a 3 Gbps GMSL link with the power + I2C on the same line.

I developed a device that connects six cameras, provides controlled filtered power, setup cameras via I2C, and receive 3 Gbps pixel data. This data stream converts to MIPI CSI (MAX9286 IC) for Nvidia Jetson TX2 through twenty 500 Mbps LVDS links.
Battery power goes via monolithic power conditioner, channel filters, and FETs.

I finished it in just two weeks, it did not need any debug, and was deployed to many cars. It was featured in the Russian hobbyist's magazine "Popular mechanics".

2018

RTK-GNSS receiver for Yandex Self-driving car

GNSS receiver board with Intel Galileo mounting on the bottom with RTK algorithms running on it. We bought an RTK-GNSS receiver before but were never satisfied with the signal level, data quality, and mostly the price.

I have developed a substitution board and precisely routed the UHF RF part to ensure the lowest signal losses, resulting in +5 dB signal level improvement. As an extra feature, USB has been added for FW updates and debugging.

The better matching with an antenna and filtering also reduced external USB3 interferences, and eliminated noise. Also I tried some RTK algorythms on this board.

2016

My hobby projects
2020

LiDAR

My first experiment on LiDAR ToF techniques with high power pulsed IR laser diode and the Avalanche photodiode with high voltage offset. Extra short laser pulses formed with STM32G0 high-resolution PWM, and light flight time was measured by the ToF IC.

A custom power source was designed to provide extra low inductance energy storage for the laser circuitry, and the other source provided the high voltage for APD offset. Other sources provided low-noise power for analog circuitry. I 3D printed a lens mount.

An unusual PCB design ensures minimal voltage overshoot on each laser pulse. It was a complicated project due to low-ESL components and tricky schematics.

2019

Smart home CAN system and sensors mesh.

I liked CAN bus through all of my vehicle electronics experience: it is a fast and reliable link with built-in priority. Of course, it is my Smart home automation bus.

It have the gate to home Ethernet and server running on Node.js. I did a scripting engine in an event style that allows to run any action at any trigger and supports composite nested sync and async actions. Like "Morning weighing triggers DB storing and weather report + quote of the day + financial info goes to the thermal printer".

Sensors data also goes through Prometheus requests to Grafana data pool + some processing + alerts. It works with Mail.ru smart speaker and controlled by voice too.
